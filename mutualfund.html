Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Lump Sum and SIP investment?

Lump Sum is a one-time investment where you invest a large amount at once. SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) involves investing a fixed amount regularly (usually monthly) over a period of time.

How is the return calculated?

For Lump Sum investments, we use compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r)^t. For SIP, we calculate monthly compounding returns on each installment. The calculations assume returns are reinvested.

Are these returns guaranteed?

No, mutual fund returns are not guaranteed and depend on market performance. This calculator provides estimates based on your expected return rate input. Past performance doesn't guarantee future returns.

Should I choose Lump Sum or SIP?

It depends on your investment goals and available capital. SIP helps in averaging out market volatility and is suitable for regular income earners. Lump Sum might be better when you have a large amount and believe it's a good time to invest.
