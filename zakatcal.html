    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is Zakat?

    Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, an obligatory charity paid annually by Muslims who meet the necessary criteria of wealth (Nisab). It purifies wealth and helps those in need.

    How does this calculator work?

    The calculator sums your eligible assets (cash, gold, investments etc.), subtracts debts/liabilities, and calculates 2.5% of the remaining amount if it meets the Nisab threshold (equivalent to 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver).

    Which assets are included in Zakat calculation?

    Zakat is due on: cash (including bank balances), gold and silver (whether jewelry or bullion), business inventory, investments, and receivables. Personal items like homes, cars, and clothing are exempt.

    How often should I pay Zakat?

    Zakat is due once every lunar year (Hijri year) when your wealth exceeds the Nisab threshold. Many Muslims pay it during Ramadan for greater blessings.

    Is my data stored or shared?
